# Debrief v1.9.3 — Redaction leading-edge privacy fix

This maintenance release prevents the first phoneme or leading edge of a
redacted word from remaining audible during in-app playback and screen recording.

## What changed

- Expanded audio protection from the old 150 ms symmetric pad to 750 ms before
  and 250 ms after each stored redaction timestamp.
- The larger leading margin covers transcription-provider timestamp drift and
  the roughly 300 ms of media time the player can cross between checks at 4×
  playback.
- Play now applies redaction volume before starting audio.
- Skip buttons, scrubber changes, chapters, comments, transcript cards, and
  search-result jumps apply mute before seeking into a protected range.
- Padded ranges clamp at the beginning of the file, normalize reversed bounds,
  merge when they overlap, and are calculated only when redactions change.
- Transcript masking remains exactly `[redacted]`; source audio and raw
  transcript data remain unchanged.

## How to use

No workflow changed:

1. Keep the Review shield on.
2. Long-press a transcript card and tap **Redact**.
3. Play or seek through the recording.

Debrief starts muting before the provider's stored first-word timestamp so the
word's onset is protected. Turn the shield off to hear and display the original
content again.

## Limitations and edge cases

- The 750 ms leading margin is intentionally privacy-first. It can mute part of
  a nearby unredacted word or immediately preceding speech.
- The 250 ms tail can similarly mute a small amount just after a redaction.
- Redaction mode is reversible app metadata, not a destructive audio editor.
  It protects playback and screen recordings made inside Debrief but does not
  create a permanently sanitized source file.
- Provider timestamps can still be inaccurate by unusual amounts. If private
  speech is still audible, redact the adjacent transcript card as well and keep
  the shield on while sharing.
- Android 10 or newer is required. The universal APK includes ARM64 and is
  verified for 16 KB memory pages used by the OnePlus 13.
- GitHub APKs are sideloaded, so Play Protect may show an unknown-developer
  warning. Install only the APK attached to this repository's release.

## Install and compatibility

Download `Debrief-v1.9.3.apk` and open it on the phone. It is production-signed
to upgrade v1.9.2 in place without removing recordings, transcripts, settings,
comments, sets, or redactions.
